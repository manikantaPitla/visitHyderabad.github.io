Nehru Zoological Park

Nehru Zoological Park is a sprawling zoo located on the outskirts of Hyderabad. It is home to a wide variety of animals, including tigers, lions, elephants, and monkeys. Visitors can also see exotic birds and reptiles, and take a safari ride through the park to see animals in their natural habitats.

The zoo was established in 1959 and was named after Jawaharlal Nehru, the first Prime Minister of India. It covers an area of 380 acres and is one of the largest zoos in India.

The zoo has over 1500 animals from 150 different species, including mammals, birds, reptiles, and amphibians. Some of the most popular animals at the zoo include lions, tigers, bears, elephants, giraffes, zebras, rhinoceroses, and primates.

The zoo has several exhibits that allow visitors to observe the animals up close. Some of the popular exhibits include the Lion Safari Park, the Natural History Museum, the Nocturnal Animal House, the Primate House, and the Reptile House.

More about Nehru Zoological Park

Butterfly Park: The zoo has a dedicated Butterfly Park, which is home to several species of butterflies. Visitors can walk through the park and observe the butterflies in their natural habitat.

Safari Options: In addition to the Lion Safari Park, the zoo also has a Deer Safari Park, which allows visitors to take a ride through the park and see different species of deer up close.

Shows: The zoo has several shows that are held throughout the day, including the Bird Show, the Elephant Show, and the Lion and Tiger Show. These shows provide visitors with a unique opportunity to see the animals in action and learn more about their behavior.

Events: The zoo hosts several events throughout the year, such as the Annual Bird Festival, which attracts bird enthusiasts from all over the country. The zoo also celebrates various festivals such as Diwali and Christmas with decorations and special events.

Conservation: The Nehru Zoological Park is committed to conservation and has several programs in place to protect endangered species. The zoo also conducts research and provides education programs to raise awareness about animal conservation.

Facilities: The zoo has several facilities for visitors, including restaurants, souvenir shops, and picnic areas. The park is also accessible for people with disabilities.

Feeding Programs: Visitors can participate in feeding programs for certain animals, such as elephants and giraffes, where they can feed the animals under the supervision of zoo staff.

Educational Programs: The zoo has several educational programs, such as the Junior Zookeeper Program, where children can learn about animal care and conservation. The zoo also has a mobile education unit that visits schools and communities to raise awareness about wildlife conservation.
