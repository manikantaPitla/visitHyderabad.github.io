Chowmahalla Palace

Chowmahalla Palace is a historical palace located in the old city of Hyderabad, Telangana, India. It was built between 1857 and 1869 by Nizam Salabat Jung. The palace served as the seat of the Asaf Jahi dynasty, the rulers of Hyderabad State, for almost 200 years. The palace was the center of Hyderabad's administrative and cultural life during the Nizam's reign. The palace was also the venue for the famous Durbar celebrations, which were held on special occasions.

Chowmahalla Palace was the official residence of the Nizams of Hyderabad while they ruled the state. The palace was built over a period of several decades, starting in the late 18th century, and was used as a center of administrative and cultural activity during the Nizam's reign

The palace was also home to the Nizam's private collection of books and manuscripts, which were stored in the Khilwat Library. The library had a rare collection of Persian and Arabic manuscripts, some of which were over 300 years old.

Architecture

Chowmahalla Palace is an excellent example of the Nizam's architecture, which is a blend of Indo-Saracenic and European styles. The palace complex consists of four palaces, each with its own unique style and design. The palaces are known as Afzal Mahal, Tahniyat Mahal, Mahtab Mahal, and Aftab Mahal. The palace complex covers an area of 12 acres and has 45 rooms, 19 courtyards, and 2 fountains.

The palace's main entrance, known as the Khilwat Mubarak, is a magnificent example of Indo-Saracenic architecture. It features intricate carvings, beautiful arches, and exquisite stucco work. The Khilwat Mubarak is also home to the grand Durbar Hall, which was used for the coronation of the Nizams and other important events.

The palace also has a beautiful collection of vintage cars, including a Rolls Royce, a Packard, and a Jaguar. The palace's interior features ornate wooden ceilings, stained glass windows, and intricate carvings. The palace's gardens are also a beautiful sight, with lush green lawns, beautiful fountains, and fragrant flowers.

Today, Chowmahalla Palace is open to the public and is a popular tourist destination in Hyderabad. The palace has been restored and renovated, and visitors can explore the palace's many rooms, courtyards, and gardens to experience the grandeur and opulence of the Nizams' lifestyle.

Interesting facts about Chowmahalla Palace

  • The palace's name, Chowmahalla, means "four palaces" in Urdu. As mentioned earlier, the palace complex consists of four palaces, each with its own unique design and architecture.

  • The palace has been used as a film set for several movies, including the Bollywood film "Jodhaa Akbar" and the Telugu film "Magadheera".

  • The palace has a Clock Tower, which was added to the palace complex in the early 20th century. The Clock Tower is 75 feet tall and features a clock face on each of its four sides.

  • Chowmahalla Palace also has a collection of vintage photographs and artifacts on display, giving visitors a glimpse into Hyderabad's rich history and culture.
